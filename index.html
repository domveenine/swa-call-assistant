Disability Seat Requirement

1. Seating Accommodation Quick Reference

How Customers Might Describe a Seating Need

  • "I need to sit up front because of my disability."
  • "I need to be by the lavatory."
  • "I can’t walk far."
  • "I’ll be getting up a lot."
  • "My child has a disability and we need to sit together."
  • "I have a bad back, can I sit in the aisle?"
  • "I used to preboard to sit by the window."

2. What To Ask

Use:

  • "Can you share what specific seating you require?"
  • "Can you share more about your seating need?"
  • "How would that seat accommodate you?"
  • "What specific seating need does your travel partner or child have?"

Avoid:

  • Asking what their disability is.
  • Asking for proof.
  • Asking what is "wrong."

3. What To Say

Do say:

  • "Based on your stated seating needs, I can reserve seat X for you."
  • "Based on what you explained, I’m unable to reserve that seat without the required charge."
  • "I’m happy to check availability based on your needs."

Avoid saying:

  • "Your disability doesn’t qualify you for that seat."
  • "Your disability doesn’t qualify you for a free seat."
  • "Do you need a special seat?"
  • "I’m sorry to hear you have that disability."

4. Core Rules To Remember

  • A Customer’s disability does not qualify their entire travel party for accommodation seating.
  • Only companions who actively assist with the disability may qualify.
  • Non qualifying companions can purchase any available seat or wait until 24 hours before departure.
  • Disability does not guarantee Extra Leg Room or seating outside fare class.
  • Customer CARE will not move another booked Customer to accommodate someone else.
  • Operational needs may require reseating.
  • You may assign any seat within the Customer’s fare class if it meets their need.
  • Allergy note: Animal or peanut allergies do not qualify for disability seating.

5. Disability Seating Blocks

Each aircraft has eight accessible seats reserved for Customers with disabilities.

-700 Aircraft

  • Bulkhead: 1A, 1B, 1C, 1D
  • Other: 7B, 7C, 14D, 14E

-800 / -8 Aircraft

  • Bulkhead: 1D, 2A, 2B, 2C
  • Other: 7B, 7C, 13D, 13E

6. Who Qualifies for Bulkhead Seating

Bulkhead seats are reserved only for:

  • A fused or immobilized leg (cannot bend).
  • A service dog not in a carrier.
  • Lift or transfer assistance onto the aircraft.
  • A companion who provides disability-related assistance.

Note: On -800 aircraft, bulkhead aisles do not have movable armrests.

7. Portable Oxygen Concentrators (POCs)

  • Customers using an approved POC on a Basic fare qualify for a Customer CARE seat assignment.
  • They must be assigned a window seat.
  • If they are assigned incorrectly, Ground Ops will move them to any available window seat.
  • If no window seats are available, Ground Ops will reseat another Customer in that row to open one.

8. When the Required Seat Is Not Available

If the Customer needs a specific seat and qualifying seats are all taken: Offer the next available flight to the same destination (co-terminals allowed), at the same fare, with the required seat confirmed on the new flight.

Bag Policy

Checked & Carry-on Allowances

  • 1st Bag = $35
  • 2nd Bag = $45
  • 3rd Bag(+) = $150
  • Each pax is allowed 1 Carry-On and 1 Personal item at no charge.

Size & Weight Allowances

  • Carry-On = (24" L) × (16" W) × (10" H)
  • Checked = 62 linear inches (L + W + H) and up to 50 pounds

Oversize & Overweight Fees

  • Overweight (51–70 pounds) = $100
  • Overweight (71–100 pounds) = $200
  • Oversize (≤80") = $200
  • Oversize & Overweight (≤100 pounds + ≤80") = $200

Contract of Carriage

Per our Contract of Carriage, passengers are prohibited from booking multiple reservations that depart from the same city on the same day or involve overlapping flight times, where it would be impossible to travel on both flights. As a result, any such reservations may be canceled without prior notice. This practice is considered double booking and violates Southwest’s Contract of Carriage.

Apple Pay / PayPal

When you use Apple Pay or PayPal to purchase a reservation on our platforms, your payment goes to Apple Pay or PayPal first, and they process it before sending the funds to Southwest. This means we do not have control over how long it takes for your financial institution to complete the transaction, and this varies for every customer and every transaction.

Companion Change

You can change your Companion up to three times within the calendar year. After the third change, the final designated companion will remain for the rest of the year.

Compliance

In-Cabin Pets

  • Pet fare: $125 each way per pet carrier, paid at the ticket counter.
  • Hawaii exception: Pets are not accepted to/from Hawaii; however, dogs and cats are permitted on inter-island flights for $35 each way per pet carrier.
  • One carrier per Customer; the carrier counts as a carryon or personal item.
  • Pet carrier must fit under the seat, and pets must remain fully inside the carrier (including head and tail) for the entire flight.
  • More information is available at Southwest.com.

Service Animals (SVANs)

  • Only service dogs trained to perform tasks for a person with a disability are accepted.
  • A DOT Service Animal Transportation Form must be presented at the ticket counter or gate on the day of travel.
  • CATIS (Customer Advised To Inform Station) applies.
  • More information is available at Southwest.com.

Unaccompanied Minor (UM)

  • Applies to children ages 5–11 traveling without a Passenger 12 years or older.
  • Must present a birth certificate or valid ID showing date of birth.
  • Fee: $100 each way ($35 each way for Hawaii inter-island travel).
  • Travel is limited to domestic nonstop or direct flights only.
UM Drop-Off Requirements
  • When dropping off a UM, the escort must: Show ID and obtain an escort pass at the ticket counter.
  • Arrive at the gate at least 45 minutes prior to departure.
  • Remain in the gate area until the flight is airborne.
UM Pick-Up Requirements
  • When picking up a UM, the escort must: Show ID and obtain an escort pass at the ticket counter.
  • If the ticket counter is closed, they may visit the BSO (Baggage Service Office).
  • Arrive at the gate at least 45 minutes prior to arrival.

Young Traveler (YT)

  • Applies to travelers ages 12–17 traveling without a Passenger 18 years or older.
  • Verify Parent/Guardian information in ARD.
  • Confirm the itinerary is domestic only (no international travel permitted).

Lap Child

  • Proof of age may be required. Acceptable proof includes an original or photocopied government-issued ID (e.g., birth certificate).
  • A Boarding Verification Document (BVD) must be issued at a kiosk, ticket counter, or digitally via the mobile app or desktop.
  • The BVD allows the child to pass through security and ensures they appear on the flight manifest.

Customers with Disabilities

  • Advise CATIS (Customer Advised To Inform Station).
  • Document the reservation accurately.

No Show

  • Reservations must be canceled at least 10 minutes before departure to avoid forfeiture of funds.
  • This includes bookings created with Points, RTFs, and SLVs.

Rapid Rewards 100% Verification

A 100% verification is required if the Customer is not a 1:1 or multi-match in C360, or is redeeming points from another Member’s account. Verify using the following:

  • Keyword (if applicable)
  • First and Last Name on the account (if not already provided)
  • Email or full mailing address, including ZIP code
  • Date of Birth of the Member
  • Last date and city pairs of travel
  • Last date and type of Partner activity
